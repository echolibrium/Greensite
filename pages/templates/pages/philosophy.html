Philosophy

No movement + Diversification = Maximum Efficiency

No movement

For decades, academics and the top investment practitioners such as John Bogle, Warren Buffett, and David Swensen, have extolled the virtues of low cost, buy-and-hold investing as the best way to protect wealth and grow it at the fastest rate.

Outperforming the market is something that very few have been able to do over time and for those that have, the average winning advantage is very small while the average losing margin is significant. These poor results are made much worse after accounting for the higher taxes associated with most active strategies while the average losing margin of those who attempt to outperform is significant.

Even the hedge fund industry, assumed by many to be a source of investment talent and therefore certain outsized profits, has delivered profits primarily to themselves and the brokerage firms, not their clients.

Source: Forbes

Broadly diversified holdings

The custom asset allocations we engineer for clients are timeless in their construction (based on research in over 45 countries, in every major asset class, and across over 140 years of data) and all based on our core tenet of diversification. We invest across every major asset class and every portfolio is custom designed after gathering specific client goals and existing conditions to require minimal movement after implementation in an effort to maximize returns to clients and minimize costs and taxes.

By minimizing movement within our portfolios we maximize tax efficiency, which includes deferring gains. The best way to defer gains for longer is by purchasing securities that merit holding for the long term. Commingled funds don't meet this criteria, as they can raise their fees, merge with another fund, or expose us to taxes through the actions of other investors. Implementing our portfolios using individual securities eliminates exposure to these risks and allows more flexibility with tax management including realizing losses to offset gains and gifting appreciated securities.
